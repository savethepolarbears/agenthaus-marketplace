# Social Media Best Practices Reference — 2026

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Threads (Meta)
- **Character limit**: 500 characters
- **Image support**: Up to 10 images; images significantly outperform text-only posts
- **Links**: Supported but penalized in reach; always put links in first comment instead
- **Hashtags**: Not supported — use topic tags for discoverability
- **Best practices**: Conversational tone, opinion-driven hooks, punchy paragraphs, authentic voice
- **Top formats**: Curated lists, local storytelling, debate prompts, utility micro-guides
- **Posting frequency**: Daily is optimal for brand building; 1-3x/day maximum

### Facebook Pages
- **Character limit**: 63,206 (but keep under 500 for engagement)
- **Link posts**: Use first-comment linking strategy — organic reach for link posts is suppressed
- **Images**: 1200×630px recommended; native uploads outperform links with previews
- **Video**: Reels format now primary; vertical (9:16) preferred
- **Best practices**: Strong first 2 lines (they appear before "See more"), clear single CTA, engage in comments
- **Engagement drivers**: Questions, polls, relatable stories, "save this" content

### Instagram
- **Feed posts**: Square (1:1) or portrait (4:5); 2200 character caption limit
- **Carousels**: Up to 20 slides; highest save rate of any format
- **Reels**: 9:16 vertical, 720p minimum, up to 90 seconds (sweet spot: 15-30s)
- **Stories**: 9:16, 24-hour lifespan, great for real-time content
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant hashtags (not 30); place in caption, not comments
- **Best practices**: Utility carousels drive saves; scenic-only posting is no longer sufficient for growth; push planning and how-to content

### Pinterest
- **Pin size**: 2:3 aspect ratio (1000×1500px ideal)
- **Titles**: Searchable, descriptive (not generic inspirational)
- **Links**: Direct article links are critical — Pinterest's primary value is traffic driving
- **Best practices**: Evergreen content performs best; clean design; text overlay on images; rich pins for articles
- **SEO matters**: Pinterest is a visual search engine — use keywords in titles and descriptions

### LinkedIn (Company/Personal)
- **Character limit**: 3000 for posts
- **Content type**: Authority, thought leadership, business insights — NOT travel inspiration
- **Best practices**: Personal stories outperform corporate content; first 2 lines are the hook; no emojis in professional contexts
- **Frequency**: 2-3x/week maximum for company pages; daily for personal profiles

### Google Business Profile
- **Post types**: Updates, offers, events
- **Best practices**: Write like a local recommendation, not a social caption; include CTAs; keep concise
- **Frequency**: 2x/week minimum for local SEO benefit

## Universal Content Rules

1. **One CTA per post** — save OR comment OR click OR send, not all four
2. **Platform-native content** — never copy-paste across networks
3. **Utility > Inspiration** — helpful content gets saved, shared, and drives return visits
4. **Strong hooks** — first line must stop the scroll
5. **Real data from real people** — collate experiences from Reddit, Facebook groups, TikTok, reviews
6. **Consistency over virality** — reliable daily posting builds more value than occasional viral hits

## Content That Drives Traffic (Niche Site Lady Framework)

Based on the NSL methodology in the project files:

1. **Collate real people's data** — gather opinions, experiences, warnings from Facebook groups, Reddit, review sites, TikTok
2. **Create must-read angles** — "X scams catching tourists", "overpriced attractions to skip", "secrets locals keep"
3. **FOMO-inducing titles** — rules, warnings, things people miss
4. **No keyword research needed** — write about what's interesting; let social distribution do the work
5. **ChatGPT for data collation** — feed it 100 comments, get organized themes and statistics
6. **Cross-platform arbitrage** — TikTok content repackaged for Facebook's older audience

## Caption Writing Framework

### The 3-Line Formula (Static Posts)
1. **Vivid hook** — one line that creates curiosity or emotion
2. **Useful detail** — one line of practical value
3. **Single CTA** — one line asking for one action

### The Promise-Deliver-Save Formula (Carousels)
1. **Slide 1**: Promise the benefit ("3 beaches tourists don't know about")
2. **Slides 2-N**: Deliver the value (one beach per slide with details)
3. **Last slide**: Save CTA ("Save this for your trip")

### The Contrast-Vote Formula (Comparison/Debate)
1. **Open with contrast** — "Oia vs Imerovigli: the sunset debate"
2. **Present both sides** with genuine pros/cons
3. **End with vote CTA** — "Which would you choose? Comment below"
